WHY 2:1 AND 3:1 RATIOS DESTROY RETURNS — PHYSICS + ECONOMICS EXPLANATION
21
Battery undersizingAt 2:1 (100 MW solar / 50 MWh battery), the battery fills up mid-morning and cannot absorb afternoon curtailment. You capture only ~30% of available curtailed energy vs. 85%+ at 1.2:1.
22
IRR mathAdding more panels at fixed battery size does NOT increase capture — it just increases curtailment and CAPEX. Each additional MW of panel at 3:1 adds $1M CAPEX but <$20K incremental revenue → IRR collapses.
23
Ramp rate mismatch (Marlon's physics)Battery must absorb solar ramp AND discharge at 7 PM peak. At 3:1, the battery cycles 2–3x/day, rapidly degrading cycle life. Effective lifetime falls from 10 yrs to 5–6 yrs, destroying NPV.
24
Grid stability economicsFrequency regulation revenue requires the battery to keep reserve capacity. Oversized solar forces the battery to be fully charged (curtailment), eliminating frequency regulation headroom.
